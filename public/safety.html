EchoStream – Child Safety Standards

CSAE / Child Safety Policy

This page describes EchoStream’s standards, policies, and reporting procedures related to child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE).

1. Zero-Tolerance Policy

EchoStream has a strict zero-tolerance policy for any content involving child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or exploitation of minors.

  • Sexual content involving minors
  • Grooming, solicitation, or exploitation
  • Requests for CSAM or sexualized images of minors
  • Any attempt to direct users to off-platform CSAM

2. Reporting In-App

Users can report content directly in the EchoStream app using the Report option on posts or profiles. Reports are reviewed promptly by moderation systems and administrators.

3. External Reporting

If a child is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services. Suspected CSAM may also be reported to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC):

https://www.missingkids.org/gethelpnow/cybertipline

4. Enforcement

EchoStream may remove content, suspend accounts, or permanently ban users who violate child safety policies. Severe violations may be reported to relevant authorities as required by law.
